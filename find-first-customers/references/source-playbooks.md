# Public Source Playbooks

Use several source types and adapt wording to the audience's language.

## Public discussions

Search forums, public communities, social posts, and replies for first-person problem statements, explicit requests, manual workarounds, and failed alternatives. Open the original discussion and capture its date and context.

Query patterns:

- `"looking for" <outcome or tool category>`
- `"alternative to" <competitor>`
- `<workflow> "manual" OR "spreadsheet" OR "takes hours"`
- `<problem> "how do you" OR "what do you use"`

## Reviews and marketplaces

Use public product reviews, app marketplaces, and comparison pages to find missing features, reliability failures, pricing objections, and switching language. Attribute the signal to the displayed public identity only.

## GitHub and technical sources

Use public issues, discussions, feature requests, migration notes, and repositories when the ICP is technical. Distinguish a maintainer, contributor, and commenter; do not assume purchasing authority.

## Company triggers

Use official company pages, changelogs, public job posts, launch announcements, integration pages, and regulatory notices. A trigger alone supports `trigger-present`; it does not prove pain or intent.

## Contact routes

Prefer the route naturally connected to the signal:

- Reply to the public thread
- Official company form
- Intentionally published business email
- Public professional profile
- Official company page

Use `none-found` rather than guessing. Do not discover personal emails, phone numbers, addresses, or private profiles.

## Source rejection rules

Reject sources that are gated, scraped without provenance, anonymous without usable context, duplicated from another page, too vague to support qualification, or prohibited by the source's access rules.
